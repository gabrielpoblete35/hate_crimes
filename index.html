Hate crimes in NYC by police precinct

By Gabriel Poblete

MIAMI -- NYPD numbers confirmed hate crime incidents are occurring more frequently in Lower Manhattan and nearby neighborhoods in Brooklyn.

652 confirmed incidents of hate crimes have been confirmed from January 2019 through September 2020, according to open data website. 208 of those incidents have occurred this year.

The NYC Mayor’s Office of Criminal Justice had reported a 15% increase from 2018 to 2019.

Last year, the city council approved creating the Office for the Prevention of Hate Crimes. Deborah Lauter, the executive director, said in a press release that the office is fully staffed and ready to go.

She pointed to the need to pay attention to NYC’s Jewish communities, “because of the increase in hate crimes motivated by anti-Semitism, particularly targeting the religious Jewish communities in Brooklyn…”

One of the goals of the office is to address the problem of underreporting such incidents.
